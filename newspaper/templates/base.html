DATA IN NEWS

This indicator model is developed to assess the use and engagement of statistics in Nepali news portals. The model is largely a contextualization of IASE 2016 Roundtable Paper proposed by Thilo Klein, Anais Galdin and El Iza Mohamedou. The paper argues that the writing of news reporters can be seen as an image for a nation's demand for statistical facts and critical analysis. The data source for the model is Nepali news portals that support RSS feeds and are published in English. The news portals that fit these criteria are The Himalayan Times, Onlinekhabar, Nepali Times, Telegraph Nepal, Kathmandu Tribune and Lokaantar. Starting from the 9th of December 2019, more than {{total_articles}} news articles were collected and analysed.

The results show that very few news articles indicate the source of data or mention development indicators. However, articles that discuss data, reports, research, statistics, and related topics do a good job of critically engaging with these ideas. These articles use keywords that can be used to assess the quality of research studies. But, the analysis also points out that the articles do not mathematically engage with the topics of data and statistics. The keywords that suggest the use of mathematical discourse to scrutinize the ideas of data and statistics in Nepali news is missing.

Level 1 Analysis

For each of the six news portals, the number of news articles which have at least one keyword indicating the presence of data source is calculated. Some examples of keywords in this context are household survey, population census, geospatial data etc. Similarly, the number of news articles with keywords indicating statistical indicators and keywords from the list of statistical capacity building projects is calculated. Some examples are indicators used in the analysis in the context of Nepal are GDP, GNP, HDI, WDI, CDI etc.

Level 2 Analysis

For each of the six news portals, articles that directly referenced data, records, research, statistics, or studies were collected. Among these articles, the share of articles that included at least one keyword that indicates critical engagement with the topics of data and statistics was calculated. Examples of keywords included ambiguous, error, bias, fake, impartial, precise, scientific, unreliable.

Level 3 Analysis

For each of the six news portals, articles that directly referenced data, records, research, statistics, or studies were collected. Among these articles, the share of articles that included at least one keyword that indicates mathematical engagement with the topics of data and statistics was calculated. Examples of keywords included data manipulation, lead question, report bias, sample select, sample size.

LIMITATIONS AND NEXT STEPS

Several limitations of the model in the context of data collection and analysis methodology have been identified.

First of all, very few Nepali news portals support news categories or webpage based RSS. We found only 6 in English when we searched for RSS between 9th December 2019- 9th January 2020. Besides, the portals that support RSS do not update their feeds on a regular basis.

Additionally, the only language medium supported currently is English. The model fails to capture segments of several news portals and a significant population size who consume news in Nepali. The model also makes the assumption that sequence matching and keywords association implies statistical literacy. This leads to a high rate of false-positives and false-negatives are high.

However, there are several plans being envisioned to mitigate these limitations. First and foremost, the data collection process is always ongoing. The backend of this dashboard collects data automatically on a daily basis. This implies, over a period of time, the sample size will be large enough for more accurate results.

Similarly, a plan for dialogue with more news portals is in motion to request RSS support. Lastly, to capture the segmentation of population who consume news in Nepali, a research and implementation plan is being developed to update the model to support the language.
