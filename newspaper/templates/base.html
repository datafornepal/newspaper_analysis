DATA IN NEWS

How journalists use data in their reporting or in their writing can be viewed as a reflection of the country’s demand for statistics and data. The World Bank team developed an indicator model to assess the use of data by Nepali news portals based on the methodology proposed by Klein, Galdin, and Mohamedou 2016. As of 9th February 2020, the results show that very few news articles indicate the source of data or mention development indicators. However, articles that discuss data, reports, research, statistics, and related topics do critically engage with these ideas.

More than {{total_articles}} articles were analyzed to assess the use of data in media (the data collection is ongoing and the analysis is updated regularly on this portal). The data source for the model was Nepali news portals that support RSS feeds and are published in English. The news portals that meet these criteria are the Himalayan Times, Onlinekhabar, the Nepali Times, the Telegraph Nepal, the Kathmandu Tribune, and Lokaantar.

Level 1 Analysis

The team analyzed whether articles indicated the presence of a data source, a statistical/development indicator, or keywords from papers on statistical capacity–building projects. As of 9th February 2020, the share of news articles that included at least one keyword that indicated “consistent non-critical” use of data averaged 7.6 percent (scores ranged from 2 percent to 12 percent).

The paper by Klein, Galdin, and Mohamedou describes "Consistent Non-critical" (Level 1) as "Appropriate but non-critical engagement with context, multiple aspects of terminology usage"

Level 2 Analysis

This level of analysis assessed whether an article critically engaged with topics of data or statistics or made arguments by using mathematical concepts. As of 9th February 2020, seventeen percent of the articles directly referenced data, records, research, statistics, or studies. Two-thirds of those articles included keywords.

The paper by Klein, Galdin, and Mohamedou describes "Critical" (Level 2) as "Critical, questioning engagement in contexts that do not involve proportional reasoning, but which do involve appropriate use of terminology."

Level 3 Analysis

“Critical mathematical” use of data occurs when an article indicates questioning or interpreting of data and statistics. As of 9th February 2020, no article met this criterion.

The paper by Klein, Galdin, and Mohamedou describes "Critical Mathematical" (Level 3) as "Critical, questioning engagement with context, using proportional reasoning particularly in chance contexts, showing appreciation of the need for uncertainty in making predictions, and interpreting subtle aspects of language."

LIMITATIONS AND NEXT STEPS

Several limitations of the model in the context of data collection and analysis methodology have been identified.

First of all, very few Nepali news portals support news categories or webpage based RSS. We found only 6 in English when we searched for RSS between 9th December 2019- 9th January 2020. Besides, the portals that support RSS do not update their feeds on a regular basis.

Additionally, the only language medium supported currently is English. The model fails to capture segments of several news portals and a significant population size who consume news in Nepali. The model also makes the assumption that sequence matching and keywords association implies statistical literacy. This leads to a high rate of false-positives and false-negatives are high.

However, there are several plans being envisioned to mitigate these limitations. First and foremost, the data collection process is always ongoing. The backend of this dashboard collects data automatically on a daily basis. This implies, over a period of time, the sample size will be large enough for more accurate results.

Similarly, a plan for dialogue with more news portals is in motion to request RSS support. Lastly, to capture the segmentation of population who consume news in Nepali, a research and implementation plan is being developed to update the model to support the language.
